Empowerment & Restoration Research Park

Planting a single tree is easy. But how do you restore vast, degraded forests and bring back as many of the locally lost species as possible? Which restoration methods are most effective in different ecosystems and different levels of degradation? How can regrowing forests withstand and mitigate the complex challenges of the climate crisis? How can restoration be maximally beneficial to the local community?
Many important scientific questions remain unanswered. And restoration initiatives around the world are waiting for answers.

As an NGO that focuses on planting trees, we have been leading a variety of research initiatives to better understand the impact of our efforts on the environment, local communities, and the broader ecosystem. Here are some examples of the research we have been conducting:


  • Ecological impact: We have been conducting research to understand the ecological impact of our tree planting initiatives, such as changes in soil moisture, air quality, and biodiversity. Our research has shown that our tree planting efforts are having a positive impact on the environment and the local ecosystem.
  • Community engagement: We have been leading research to understand the role of local communities in planting and maintaining trees, and to identify the benefits and challenges of involving communities in these efforts. Our research has shown that community engagement is crucial to the success of our tree planting initiatives and has helped us to build strong partnerships with local communities.
  • Carbon sequestration: We have been researching the potential of our tree planting initiatives to sequester carbon and mitigate climate change. Our research has shown that our efforts are having a significant impact on carbon sequestration and are helping to mitigate the effects of climate change.
  • Reforestation: We have been leading research on the best practices for reforestation, including the selection of appropriate tree species, methods for planting and maintaining trees, and the impact of reforestation on local communities and ecosystems. Our research has helped us to refine our reforestation practices and to ensure that our efforts are sustainable and have a lasting impact.
  • Ecosystem services: We have been studying the ecosystem services provided by our tree planting initiatives, such as improved water quality, soil conservation, and wildlife habitat. Our research has shown that our efforts are having a positive impact on the ecosystem and are providing a range of benefits to the local community.

These are just a few examples of the research we have been leading. Our goal is to continue to expand our research efforts and to deepen our understanding of the impact of our tree planting initiatives on the environment and the local community.
